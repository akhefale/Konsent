Om Konsent

Something about hierarchy and the freedom to make decisions

It's easy to leave your choices to others; to give up free will in order to avoid the difficult decisions.

It's easy to let others take a weight from your shoulders; to elect a president, vote for a politician or donate to a non-profit. It means escaping from the scary possibility that you might make a choice that results in an unwanted outcome.

It also means giving up your freedom. Giving up your ability to develop your self, learn from your mistakes and shape your own life on the fundaments of your own beliefs. It is my opinion that it is of vital importance that we have the strength to carry our own decisions on our own shoulders - and, perhaps of even greater importance, that we realize and truly believe the fact that nobody can take decisions on our behalf better than ourselves.

The inevitable truth is that we are more or less stuck here, on planet earth, with our fellow humans, animals and nature, and therefore have to respect these entities in order to ensure our own welfare. The systems and structures that currently rule our society, are based on the belief that humans are not able to respect anything but themselves - with the exception of a few chosen individuals, who are somehow morally superior in every way, and therefore have the ability to righteously rule the masses. They are based on the idea that chaos, death and abuse will erupt if society is left without hierarchy.

Konsent is my proposal for how these destructive power structures can be abolished in smaller communities, in order to establish equal freedom for every individual. Konsent is based on old concepts such as concensus, direct democracy, flat decision-making and anarchism. Konsent is proof that communities can thrive without hierarchy.

- Adrian E. Bratlann
